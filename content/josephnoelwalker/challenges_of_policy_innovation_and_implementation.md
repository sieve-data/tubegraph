### Structural Barriers to Reform

The main reason for poor policy outcomes is not a lack of cognitive skills within the public service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:11">[00:26:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:48">[00:26:48]</a>. Instead, it stems from a democratic political system where politicians adopt a "small target strategy," offering minimal policy changes to avoid controversy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:07">[00:27:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:24">[00:27:24]</a>. This is compounded by the belief that policy proposals must have been explicitly put to the electorate *before* an election to claim a mandate for implementation, resulting in "nothing" being done once in government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:35">[00:27:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:03">[00:28:03]</a>. This lack of courage to be honest and pursue "big ideas" is observed across politics, not just in Australia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:45">[00:28:45]</a>.

Specific examples of policies that have failed to be implemented despite being well-researched and gaining stakeholder support include:
*   **Comprehensive road user charging**: Recommended 15 years ago, this policy aimed to replace fuel excise, motor vehicle registration, driver's license fees, and stamp duties on vehicles with charges based on vehicle weight, distance traveled, and potential congestion or carbon components <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:25">[00:42:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:05">[00:43:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:34">[00:43:34]</a>. Despite support from motoring and trucking groups, it remains undone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:44">[00:44:44]</a>.
*   **Mining tax**: The proposal for a mining tax faced an "extraordinarily simple" and politically potent counter-narrative: "killing the goose that's laying the golden eggs" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:09">[00:46:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:37">[00:46:37]</a>. This, despite economists knowing that the mining boom was *crowding out* other sectors of the economy through real exchange rate appreciation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:42">[00:46:42]</a>.

The "complacency" stemming from Australia's economic success due to the mining boom, particularly the favorable terms of trade since 2002, has also contributed to a lack of reform urgency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:40">[01:43:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:06">[01:44:06]</a>.

## Reforming the Tax System and Fiscal Discipline

The need for tax reform is pressing. Indexing personal income tax scales is proposed as a first step to address "fiscal drag," which has led to a reduction in real wages for average workers while their average tax rate increased <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:13">[00:53:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:52">[00:53:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:40">[00:55:40]</a>. This disproportionately impacts younger generations and undermines intergenerational equity and social cohesion, with capital income being much more favorably taxed than labor income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:57">[00:53:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:26">[00:56:26]</a>.

Furthermore, there's a strong case for a comprehensive review of Commonwealth-State roles and responsibilities, starting with expenditure. This aims to deliver better outcomes for citizens by clarifying overlapping responsibilities in areas like age care and health, which became "deplorable" during crises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:54">[02:04:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:06">[02:05:06]</a>. Such a review would naturally lead to discussions about the appropriate allocation of tax bases, with a focus on reforming "egregious" state taxes like stamp duties on property transfers and insurance taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:06:17">[02:06:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:27">[02:07:27]</a>.

The "Charter of Budget Honesty Act," a key instrument of fiscal discipline and transparency developed in the 1990s, has been "trashed by both sides of politics" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:06">[00:51:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:32">[00:51:32]</a>. Rebuilding this fiscal discipline is crucial to avoid potential negative tail risks from global market disturbances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:00">[00:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:02">[00:53:02]</a>.

## Fostering [[Innovation and Technology Development|Innovation]] and Productivity

[[Innovation Challenges and the Role of Regulation|Microeconomic reform]] primarily focuses on achieving efficiency and getting closer to a "production possibility frontier" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:12">[01:35:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:22">[01:35:22]</a>. However, sustained economic growth, particularly in GDP per capita, depends on technological progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:09">[01:36:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:13">[01:36:13]</a>. The key policy question is how to create an environment conducive to the development of technological innovation that can be commercialized domestically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:58">[01:36:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:10">[01:37:10]</a>.

Australia's "underutilized" expertise in areas like chemical engineering for critical minerals processing, and the lack of a domestic engineered wood product industry despite abundant timber, highlight missed opportunities in adding value to raw materials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:11">[01:30:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:25">[01:30:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:02">[01:32:02]</a>. This often comes down to a "mindset thing" where Australia focuses on exporting raw materials rather than processing them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:38">[01:32:38]</a>. The challenge is attracting global capital given the perception that Australia's economic success is primarily driven by mining, rather than a diversified, innovative economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:51">[01:27:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:22">[01:28:22]</a>.

## Why the Reform Era Ended

The cessation of Australia's ambitious economic reforms, which ran from 1983 to 2000, is attributed to several factors:
*   **Complacency**: Driven by the sustained positive terms of trade from the mining boom, leading to a belief that Australia is doing well without needing further significant reforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:40">[01:43:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:06">[01:44:06]</a>.
*   **Lack of Political Ambition and Courage**: Leaders like Paul Keating and John Howard pursued difficult reforms because they wanted to prove their national leadership capabilities and leave an "impressive legacy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:07">[01:45:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:20">[01:45:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:01">[01:46:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:25">[01:48:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:23">[01:49:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:41">[01:49:41]</a>. Recent politicians have expressed disinterest in "legacy" as a "vanity project" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:32">[01:49:32]</a>.
*   **"Small Target Strategy" and "Mandate"**: The political landscape discourages bold proposals, as parties offer minimal change to secure votes, and then feel constrained by a perceived lack of a pre-election mandate to implement significant policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:06">[01:39:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:08">[01:39:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:07">[02:27:07]</a>.
*   **Loss of a "Burning Platform" Narrative**: Major reforms historically required a "burning platform" â€“ a sense of urgency or crisis that galvanizes national attention and motivates action. Without such a narrative, which politicians can create, deep-seated complacency persists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:16">[02:01:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:57">[02:01:57]</a>.

Despite these challenges, there is hope. Leaders exist who are capable, but they lack a sense of urgency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:36">[02:00:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:01">[02:01:01]</a>.
