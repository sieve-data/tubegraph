## Joining the Indian Army

Captain Ragu Raman, also known as Raman, joined the Indian Army in 1986 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:58:00">[03:58:00]</a>. His decision was influenced by witnessing the 1984 Delhi riots, where the arrival of the Army brought an immediate end to the chaos, inspiring him at around 18-20 years old <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:08:00">[03:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:30:00">[03:30:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:45:00">[03:45:00]</a>.

### Selection Process
The selection process for the Indian Army is a three-stage procedure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:32:00">[07:32:00]</a>:
1.  **Common Entrance Test:** An objective-type aptitude test, which is a filtration process for applicants after schooling (NDA) or graduation (direct entry), as well as for technical entries like engineers and doctors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:34:00">[07:34:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:52:00">[07:52:00]</a>.
2.  **Services Selection Board (SSB) Interview:** A three-day process testing for "officer-like quality" or leadership qualities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:18:00">[08:18:20]</a>. This includes an intense psychometric testing to assess mental stability and suitability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:50:00">[08:50:00]</a>. The tests aim to discern between negative and positive thought patterns, for instance, how one responds to words like "knife" or "blood" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:10:00">[09:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:32:00">[09:32:00]</a>. Faking responses is difficult due to the experience of the testing team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:49:00">[09:49:00]</a>.
3.  **Final Selection and Induction:** Unselected applicants are informed that rejection doesn't signify an end to their future, and stories of successful candidates making it on multiple attempts are shared to provide encouragement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:03:00">[10:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:49:00">[10:49:00]</a>. This process is considered a masterclass in HR for its sensitivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:06:00">[10:06:00]</a>.

### Essential Qualities for a Leader
The Army looks for individuals who can lead a team, even if team members are individually superior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:36:00">[08:36:00]</a>. A critical trait is physical courage—the ability to put oneself in physical danger and remain present to calm a volatile situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:25:00">[12:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:52:00">[12:52:00]</a>. This courage also extends to endurance and maintaining spirit under interrogation, as seen in prisoners of war <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:31:00">[13:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:39:00">[13:39:00]</a>.

## Training Regimen
The training in the Indian Army, lasting about 18 months in academies like the Indian Military Academy (IMA) or Officers Training Academy (OTA), is extremely intense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:07:00">[04:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:16:00">[04:16:00]</a>. It's designed to train leaders to operate under conditions of high stress where their lives are at risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:45:00">[04:45:00]</a>.

### Pedagogy and Discipline
A key aspect of training is creating synchronized troops who act under one command <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:10:00">[06:10:00]</a>, exemplified by drills where hundreds of officers move in perfect unison, compensating for the speed of sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:20:00">[06:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:02:00">[07:02:00]</a>. This repeated drilling, even as punishment, aims to forge a unit that moves at the speed of sound, with a margin of error equivalent to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:06:00">[07:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:20:00">[07:20:24]</a>.

### Continuous Learning and Specialization
Contrary to popular belief, joining the Army involves continuous learning and numerous exams throughout one's career <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:03:00">[16:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:05:00">[18:05:00]</a>. After initial basic training, officers choose a specific arm (e.g., Infantry, Armored Core, Signals, Engineers) based on aptitude and liking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:47:00">[16:47:00]</a>. They then undergo specialized training in their chosen discipline, followed by advanced courses to become instructors for future generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:13:00">[17:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:43:00">[17:43:00]</a>.

### Commando Training and Mental Fortitude
The Commando course, a 35-day and night ordeal, is designed not just for physical fitness but primarily for [[psychological_and_mental_training_in_the_armed_forces | mental training]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:41:00">[14:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:00:00">[15:00:00]</a>. It involves rigorous physical conditioning, weapons training, demolitions, and "escape and evasion" exercises where participants must navigate back from simulated enemy territory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:19:00">[19:19:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:26:00">[19:26:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:08:00">[20:08:00]</a>. The core aim is to train the mind to solve problems under extreme stress, fear, and exhaustion, providing a psychological anchor that if one survived the Commando course, they can survive anything <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:09:00">[15:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:17:00">[15:17:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:29:00">[15:29:00]</a>.

### Operational Experiences
Captain Ragu's operational tenures included:
*   **Punjab (1987):** His first posting in Sangrur involved minor skirmishes and cleanups during a period of terrorism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:31:00">[36:31:00]</a>.
*   **Siachen:** Known as the highest and coldest battlefield in the world, altitudes range from 18,000 to 22,000 feet, with temperatures dropping to -40 to -45 degrees Celsius <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:16:00">[39:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:48:00">[39:48:00]</a>. All battalions inducted into Siachen undergo specialized training in mountaineering, rescue, climbing, and patrolling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:14:00">[41:14:00]</a>.
*   **Angola (UN Peacekeeping Mission):** As part of a UN peacekeeping contingent, Captain Ragu served as a "Blue Helmet" combat troop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:37">[01:16:37]</a>. This role differs from traditional combat as it involves inspiring troops as brand ambassadors of India and exercising immense restraint in volatile situations to de-escalate conflicts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:43">[01:14:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:50">[01:14:50]</a>. ### The Reality of Combat and Personal Growth
Soldiers generally do not look forward to war because they understand the pain and devastating consequences of a bullet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:56:00">[32:56:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:12:00">[33:12:00]</a>. However, they are eager to participate in battles because it is what they have trained for, similar to a cricketer waiting to play for their national team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:21:00">[33:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:36:00">[33:36:00]</a>. Officers on deputation often volunteer to rejoin their units during operations, feeling a deep obligation to be with their platoons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:56:00">[33:56:00]</a>.

The greatest fear for a leader in combat is not personal injury or death, but the apprehension of whether they will be able to perform their duty and bear the immense responsibility of other people's lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:08:00">[30:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:36:00">[31:36:00]</a>. The burden of potentially causing someone else's death and facing their family is a significant weight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:44:00">[31:44:00]</a>. Once an operation begins, it becomes a blur of muscle memory and training, with plans often failing upon first contact with the enemy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:02:00">[32:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:14:00">[32:14:00]</a>.

Experience in combat, or "battle hardening," is crucial as it seasons troops and allows them to operate without being phased, unlike a first-timer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:47">[01:01:47]</a>. The Army uses "battle inoculation" in training, involving live artillery and machine gun fire, to simulate combat conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:11">[01:02:11]</a>. However, there is no replacement for actual combat, as targets in real situations "shoot back" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:40">[01:02:40]</a>.

Coming out of a mission alive, especially without losing lives or with minimal casualties, provides an immense sense of relief and satisfaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:51">[01:00:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:17">[01:01:17]</a>. Surviving traumatic experiences, whether in combat or a civilian accident, fundamentally changes a person's perspective on life, making them re-evaluate what is important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:50">[00:52:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:06">[00:53:06]</a>.

Indian soldiers are renowned globally for their professionalism, intelligence, and innovative "jugaad" mentality, which allows them to improvise with limited resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:01">[01:13:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:13">[01:13:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:30">[01:13:30]</a>. ## Why Join the Indian Army Today?
Joining the Indian Army, even for a 10-year short service commission, offers unique benefits for young individuals:
*   **Leadership and Management Skills:** The Army provides firsthand experience in leadership and management, with concepts learned in business schools often having their primary source in military principles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:00">[01:26:00]</a>.
*   **Camaraderie and Wealth of Relationships:** It fosters an unparalleled sense of camaraderie and lifelong bonds, creating a network of support that is considered true wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:16">[01:26:16]</a>.
*   **Diverse Life Experiences:** The career offers unparalleled travel and exposure to different regions, cultures, and situations that one would normally never encounter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:28">[01:27:28]</a>. It provides a "richer life" filled with "highs and lows" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:17">[01:27:17]</a>.
*   **Skill Development:** Beyond combat skills, the Army teaches accelerated learning, allowing individuals to quickly grasp sophisticated technologies and complex equipment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:11">[01:32:11]</a>. This confidence in learning difficult tasks translates to other areas of life, fostering adaptability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:10">[01:33:10]</a>.
*   **Financial Discipline and Perspective:** Government services, including the Army, teach the value of living within one's means, demonstrating that happiness is not dependent on material wealth but on a sense of "enough" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:50">[01:34:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:05">[01:35:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:05">[01:38:05]</a>.
*   **Contribution to the Nation:** Retired Army signal officers, for instance, were instrumental in driving India's telecom revolution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:16">[01:43:16]</a>. The Army is seen as India's most expensive academy, and the highly trained individuals it produces, who enter the civilian workforce in the peak of their lives, are a huge national resource <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:41">[01:43:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:58">[01:43:58]</a>.
