This article explores examples of highly successful businesses operating with only one or two core individuals, often achieving millions in revenue or significant acquisition values. These "mighty businesses" prioritize leverage, often flying under the radar while maintaining high profitability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:07">[02:07:07]</a>.

The definition of a "one or two-person business" is based on the spirit of the law rather than the strict letter; it typically means the founder(s) are the core drivers, possibly utilizing contractors, agencies, or vendors for other tasks, or having started as a solo venture before limited hiring <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:46:12">[04:46:12]</a>. They generally lack a large employee base, t-shirts, or physical offices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52:00">[04:52:00]</a>.

## Notable Examples of [[innovative_oneperson_companies]]

### Biggest One-Person Business
*   **War Graphs**
    *   **Description**: A companion app for the game *League of Legends* that tracked player stats and helped improve gameplay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>.
    *   **Key Stats**: Built by one individual, it grew to approximately a million users and generated significant revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>. Sold for $54 million cash, with half upfront and the other half as an earn-out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:00">[01:29:00]</a>. The founder was estimated to be around 30 after seven or eight years of development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:00">[01:45:00]</a>.
*   **Stardew Valley**
    *   **Description**: An 8-bit style farming simulator game created by Eric Barone. He spent four years in solitude building the game, teaching himself to code after school to avoid a traditional job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:31:00">[05:31:00]</a>.
    *   **Key Stats**: Sold 20 million copies at $13 each, generating over $150 million in sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:59:00">[06:59:00]</a>. Barone was the sole developer, supported by his girlfriend's stipend and a part-time usher job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:30:00">[06:30:00]</a>.
*   **Minecraft**
    *   **Description**: A sandbox game primarily developed by one person, "Notch" (Markus Persson), who streamed himself coding it for hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:18:00">[07:18:00]</a>.
    *   **Key Stats**: Notch sold the company for billions (around $2-3 billion) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:46:00">[07:46:00]</a>.
*   **StreamYard**
    *   **Description**: A platform for streaming online events and interviews, launched in 2019 just before the pandemic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:00">[09:00:00]</a>.
    *   **Key Stats**: Two founders scaled it to $30 million in revenue in 18 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:12:00">[09:12:00]</a>. They sold it to Hopin for $250 million (mix of cash and stock) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:21:00">[09:21:00]</a>. They reached $14 million ARR with just two people before hiring a team to scale to $30 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:32:00">[10:32:00]</a>.
*   **Plenty of Fish**
    *   **Description**: An early dating website, predominantly Canadian, started by one person named Markus Frind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:30:00">[11:30:00]</a>.
    *   **Key Stats**: Acquired for approximately $550 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:33:00">[11:33:00]</a>.

## Key Takeaways and [[Strategies for Solopreneurs]]

### Common Industries
*   **Thought Leader on Social Media**: Leveraging free content to establish expertise and monetize through related services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:53">[01:07:53]</a>.
*   **E-commerce**: Many single or two-person e-commerce businesses can generate millions in profit annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:59">[01:07:59]</a>.
*   **Games**: Solo or small teams creating popular games (e.g., Stardew Valley, Minecraft) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:11">[01:08:11]</a>.
*   **Apps and Plugins**: Building valuable software add-ons for existing platforms (e.g., Google Sheets plugins, Figma plugins) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:17">[01:08:17]</a>.
*   **Data Aggregators**: Compiling and indexing data to provide valuable search capabilities or insights (e.g., BuiltWith, Milled.com, OnlyFinders) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:23">[01:08:23]</a>.

### Core Strategies
*   **Focus on Niche and Authority**: [[Utilizing unique niches for small businesses]] is crucial. Establishing oneself as an authority in a specific niche through free content or unique offerings can lead to monetization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:22:00">[31:22:00]</a>.
*   **Excellence in Distribution or Product**: Success hinges on being either an incredible influencer/content creator (distribution) or an exceptional product builder (product) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:40">[01:08:40]</a>. The complementary skill can then be outsourced (e.g., hiring a publisher for a game) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:52">[01:08:52]</a>.
*   **Expert Attributes**: These businesses require specific skills at an expert level, such as copywriting, design, investment judgment, or market analysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:12">[01:09:12]</a>.
*   **Leverage**: To achieve high revenue with minimal staff, leverage is essential. This can be achieved through:
    *   **Code**: Building software that runs automatically once created <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:44">[01:09:44]</a>.
    *   **Media**: Utilizing platforms like podcasts, newsletters, or social media to reach a wide audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:49">[01:09:49]</a>.
    *   **Capital**: Using financial resources to scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:53">[01:09:53]</a>.
*   **Global Reach**: The internet allows the "best of anything" to sell to the entire world, enabling highly specialized experts to reach a global audience (e.g., Miss Excel teaching Excel globally through TikTok) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:15">[01:10:15]</a>.
*   **Timing**: Being early to new platforms or trends (e.g., Twitter in its early days, OnlyFans growth) can provide a significant advantage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55:00">[03:55:00]</a>. This is akin to "surfing waves" â€“ being present and ready to paddle out for opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a>.

## Challenges and Downsides
*   **Loneliness and Pressure**: Running a one-person business can be incredibly lonely, lacking the camaraderie, accountability, and collaborative brainstorming opportunities found in larger teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:58">[01:10:58]</a>. The pressure to constantly perform and innovate can be immense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.
*   **Burnout**: Sustaining intense effort for extended periods, especially with high demand, can lead to burnout <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:00">[02:26:00]</a>.
*   **Value of Partnerships**: While appealing, being a "solo capitalist" or solopreneur can be less fun and effective than a partnership. A complementary co-founder or strong partner can provide valuable support, differing skill sets, and intellectual sparring for ideas, making the entrepreneurial journey more enjoyable and often more successful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:51">[01:11:51]</a>. A "duopreneur" model (two people) is often seen as a sweet spot for autonomy and effectiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:10">[01:12:10]</a>.

Overall, these [[Entrepreneurial Success Stories]] highlight that with the right combination of niche focus, leveraged execution, and relentless dedication, individuals or small teams can build immensely profitable and impactful businesses.
