## Defining Rational Thought and Science

Humanity constantly faces a bombardment of incomprehensible data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:28">[00:08:28]</a>. To cope, humans put forth assumptions or hypotheses and then test them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>.

When a result contrary to an assumption is encountered, the process involves:
*   Initially questioning one's own perception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:24">[00:10:24]</a>.
*   Next, wondering if the theory is incomplete and seeking to refine it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:32">[00:10:32]</a>.
*   Finally, if contradictions persist, acknowledging the theory might be wrong and seeking a more correct one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:42">[00:10:42]</a>.

This approach of [[religious_belief_and_rationality | falsifiable]] assumptions is the way of learning and the way of [[religious_belief_and_rationality | science]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>.
