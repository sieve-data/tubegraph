The Bazaar of Marrakech is considered the most colorful and beautiful of oriental bazaars, an "incredible endless labyrinth" of "colorful excitement," though it can be tiring to navigate through the crowds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:23]</a>.

## [[historical_discussions_of_berber_and_arab_identities | Berber Identity and Arabization]] in Morocco

The local Berber population in Morocco initially resisted Islamic rule for centuries when the Arabs arrived <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>. However, later dynasties and rulers of Morocco, although often of native Berber origin, "boasted of being of Arab origin and being more Muslim than everyone else" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:26">[00:11:26]</a>.

When Berber principalities sought to establish states and dominate broader regions, they consistently adopted "Arab culture, Arab identity and the Islamic religion as their flag" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:24">[00:12:24]</a>. This is because Arab and Islamic identity became the "symbol of civilization" and the "tool" to gain submission beyond one's own tribe or small geography <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:36">[00:12:36]</a>.

For instance, the significant empires established in the last 1000 years, dominating all of West Northwest Africa (Algeria, Tunisia) and Spain, originated from small-scale Berber areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>. Upon becoming empires, their leaders invariably claimed descent from figures like Haz Ali or Hasan, asserting their status as "sherifs" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:37">[00:14:37]</a>. This illustrates the role of Islam and Arab dominance as conditions for large-scale sovereignty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:05">[00:15:05]</a>.

### Comparison to [[comparisons_between_cultural_and_modern_elements_in_morocco_and_trkiye | Turkey]]

Being Arab has become a "complementary element of being Muslim," used as a cultural and political tool, similar to how Turkishness and Muslimness are used in Turkey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:14">[00:16:14]</a>.

### State Structure Differences
A significant difference from the Ottoman Empire is the permanence and sophistication of its state organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:41">[00:16:41]</a>. Moroccan dynasties were temporary, constantly facing legitimacy challenges and rebellions upon a new ruler's ascent or a sultan's death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:56">[00:16:56]</a>. The Moroccan state would "completely disintegrate every 30-40 years or at the latest every 100 years" before gathering again <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:48">[00:17:48]</a>.

In contrast, the Ottomans had a stable dynasty for 600 years with an institutional structure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:10">[00:18:10]</a>. This dominance was particularly strong in Western Anatolia and Rumelia, areas influenced by Byzantine political culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:12">[00:19:12]</a>. The Ottoman core, centered in Istanbul, possessed a distinct structure, attributable to its Byzantine tradition and heritage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:54">[00:19:54]</a>. However, the eastern parts of the Ottoman Empire, with Kurdish and Arab principalities, historically resembled the unstable political structure of Morocco, with frequent rebellions against tax collection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:32">[00:18:32]</a>.

## Ottoman Imperial Ambitions and [[middle_eastern_geopolitical_tensions | Global Political Dynamics]]

The Ottomans attempted to establish sovereignty in Algeria in the 16th century, though their formal administrative structure did not fully take hold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:20">[00:20:20]</a>. Local rulers submitted, and the Ottomans even captured Fez and Marrakech twice, holding onto Morocco for brief periods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:45">[00:20:45]</a>.

The primary motivation for these efforts was a "desperate" search for an Atlantic port, an "exit to the Atlantic Ocean" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:22">[00:21:22]</a>. The Ottomans, having become masters of the Eastern Mediterranean and central to medieval trade routes between China, India, and Europe, faced a crisis when the axis of world trade shifted towards the Atlantic and Indian Oceans due to European maritime successes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:49">[00:21:49]</a>.

During the mid-16th century (Sokollu Mehmet Pasha's time, Sultan Suleiman's last years, and Selim II's/Murat III's reigns), the Ottoman Empire ambitiously sent fleets to the Red Sea, Ethiopia, Abyssinia, and Gujarat (India) to fight the Portuguese and establish commercial/military relations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:03">[00:23:03]</a>. Concurrently, they sent an army via Algeria to establish a base on the Atlantic coast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:57">[00:23:57]</a>. Both these grand projects, aimed at securing a share in new world trade routes, ended in "big fiascos," with the Ottomans withdrawing from Morocco after a short period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:22">[00:24:22]</a>. This failure contributed to the "great collapse of the Ottoman Empire" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:54">[00:24:54]</a>.

Despite these historical attempts, there is "great admiration for the Ottoman Empire and Türkiye" in the entire Arab world, viewing Turkey as a "rich relative" and the "whitest Muslim country" (not counting Tatars, Bosnians, Albanians) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:02">[01:32:02]</a>. This perception has a racial element, though Islamic world is not typically seen as racist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:00">[01:33:00]</a>.

In the 17th century, Morocco's Alawite dynasty imitated the Ottomans, establishing a professional army with Turkish commanders and officers, and using the Turkish title "Pasha" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:31">[01:33:31]</a>. This title persisted, seen in historical figures like Ba Ahmet Pasha, a black African prime minister and dictator, whose palace and a street in Marrakech bear the "Pasha" name <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:21">[01:34:21]</a>.

While language, symbolic thought, and the ability to cooperate based on symbolic beings are unique human capacities, fundamental aspects like birth, childhood, adolescence, sexual life, and death are zoological and biological <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:24">[02:24:24]</a>.

He critiques recent "gender and race theories" in the Western world, particularly the claim that sexual behaviors and differences between sexes are purely learned and cultural <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:03:33">[03:03:33]</a>. This is deemed an "absurd idea" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:14:46">[03:14:46]</a>. ### Languages and Power Dynamics

The language of the "politically dominant bosses" becomes the language of the elite and is learned by those seeking to advance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:14">[04:00:14]</a>. This is exemplified by the Arabization of Morocco, where despite a 95% Berber population, Arabic became the dominant culture and language because Arab rulers were stronger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01:39">[04:01:39]</a>.

Post-1945, Western countries implemented social programs (unemployment, health insurance, limited working hours, public housing) to dilute socialism and improve living standards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:05">[04:09:05]</a>. While successful for 20-30 years, states began to go bankrupt in the 1970s, leading to the abandonment of social democratic policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:40">[04:10:40]</a>. By the 1990s, social democratic parties worldwide faced a crisis, losing credibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:27">[04:11:27]</a>. They shifted focus to environmentalism, feminism, and gay rights, which appealed to university graduates, but are now at "the point of exhaustion" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:53">[04:11:53]</a>. Thus, the Republican People's Party (CHP) cannot be social democratic, as the concept itself is an "old era" discussion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:12:31">[04:12:31]</a>.

Current dominant political platforms are the migration problem and global realignment/alliances (e.g., NATO stance, response to [[global_political_dynamics_and_conflicts | Israel]]/[[global_political_dynamics_and_conflicts | Ukraine]] crises) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:13:18">[04:13:18]</a>.
