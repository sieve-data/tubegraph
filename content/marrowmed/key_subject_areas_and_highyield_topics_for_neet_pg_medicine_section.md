## Shift in Exam Pattern

The [[neet_pg_2024_exam_analysis | NEET PG 2024]] exam marked a return to an older format, similar to exams prior to January 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>. Previously, exams in 2021, 2022, and to some extent 2023, were easier, featuring one-liner questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. The shift in 2024 suggests a change in the governing or question paper setting body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>. This new format requires candidates to handle significant pressure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>.

The new pattern involves questions presented in blocks, much like a shooting competition, where students cannot return to previous blocks of questions once completed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>. For example, a block might contain 40 questions to be answered in 42 minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>. This contrasts with older exams where all 300 questions were accessible throughout the 3.5-hour duration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>. This format emphasizes execution and time management as detrimental factors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>.

## Key Skills for Success

For the [[strategies_for_neet_pg_exam | NEET PG exam]], success hinges on specific skills:

*   **Integration:** Questions are no longer from single subjects; they are overlapping and require a good grounding in [[skills_required_to_succeed_in_neet_pg_exams | integration]] across disciplines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.
*   **Identifying the Core:** Questions are often long, with necessary and unnecessary details, images, etc. The ability to quickly identify the core of the question is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.
*   **Presence of Mind and Composure:** The timed, block-based format demands calmness and composure to avoid being overwhelmed by time pressure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>.
*   **Execution (Shooting Skills):** Rapid and accurate answering is vital, as there is no "coming back" to missed questions in previous blocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.
*   **Preparation "One Step Above":** To succeed, candidates must prepare at a level one step above what is strictly required for the exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a>.

[[strategies_for_neet_pg_preparation | Strategies for NEET PG preparation]] should involve "studying with stress," anticipating complex, integrated questions rather than relying on straightforward recall or passive note-reading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>. Early preparation, starting as early as the second or third year of MBBS, is also highly recommended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:50">[00:09:50]</a>.

## High-Yield Topics from the Recall Sessions

The [[neet_pg_2024_exam_analysis | NEET PG 2024]] exam was divided into two sessions: Session One (easier to identify the core) and Session Two (questions themselves were more difficult) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:10">[01:12:10]</a>.

### Session One Examples

*   **Endocrinology:**
    *   Thyrotoxicosis/Hyperthyroidism: Differentiation, symptoms (weight loss, tachycardia, heat intolerance, tremor), and the concept of Graves' disease as autoimmune hyperthyroidism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:32">[00:15:32]</a>.
    *   [[ophthalmology_questions_in_neet_pg_exam | Ophthalmology questions in NEET PG exam]] related to Graves' disease (ophthalmopathy), its autoimmune nature, management (steroids, decompression), and its independent course from thyrotoxicosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:13">[00:17:13]</a>.
    *   Addison's disease: Symptoms (fatigability, anemia, hyperpigmentation, hypotension, hyponatremia, hyperkalemia), primary vs. secondary adrenal insufficiency, and treatment (hydrocortisone, fludrocortisone) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:31">[00:20:31]</a>.
    *   Pheochromocytoma: Rule of 10 and association with MEN 2A <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:29">[00:23:29]</a>.
    *   Type 1 Diabetes: Stages of beta-cell damage (e.g., Stage 3: autoimmune, dysglycemia, symptomatic) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:27">[00:24:27]</a>.

*   **Gastroenterology/Hepatology:**
    *   Wilson's disease: Gold standard investigation (hepatic copper concentration via liver biopsy) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:35">[00:25:35]</a>.
    *   Celiac disease: Typical vs. atypical presentation, associated conditions (short stature, anemia, neuropathy, osteoporosis), and diagnosis (IgA TTG) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:06">[00:28:06]</a>.
    *   Acanthocytes with neurological symptoms and steatorrhea: Abetalipoproteinemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:57">[00:28:57]</a>.

*   **Hematology:**
    *   CD markers: Differentiating lymphomas (e.g., DLBCL vs. Burkitt lymphoma based on age and markers) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:34">[00:29:34]</a>.
    *   Von Willebrand disease: Types and severity (Type 3 most severe) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:07">[00:31:07]</a>.
    *   Acute leukemia: Diagnosis (low platelets, low hemoglobin) and specific types (e.g., APL with DIC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:37">[00:31:37]</a>.

*   **Nephrology:**
    *   ABG (Arterial Blood Gas) interpretation: High anion gap metabolic acidosis with partial respiratory compensation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:58">[00:32:58]</a>.
    *   ECG findings: Associated with electrolyte imbalances (hypercalcemia, hypokalemia, hyperkalemia) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:16">[00:34:16]</a>.

*   **Rheumatology/Immunology:**
    *   ANCA vasculitis: Microscopic polyangiitis (pulmonary hemorrhage) vs. Churg-Strauss syndrome (fleeting infiltrates, allergic symptoms) vs. GPA (Wegener's) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:56">[00:34:56]</a>.

*   **Respiratory System:**
    *   [[community_medicine_in_neetpg | Community Medicine]] acquired pneumonia: Diagnosis based on symptoms and X-ray <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>.
    *   Kartagener syndrome: Situs inversus, chronic sinusitis, bronchiectasis, male infertility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:14">[00:36:14]</a>.
    *   Pleural effusion: Differentiating transudative (e.g., CCF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome) vs. exudative (e.g., TB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:30">[00:36:30]</a>.
    *   Bronchial Asthma: GINA guidelines (inhaled corticosteroids as main stay, not just short-acting beta agonists) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:37">[00:37:37]</a>.
    *   Panacinar emphysema: Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:45">[00:38:45]</a>.
    *   Inflammation: Role of interleukins (IL-8 as chemotactic agent for neutrophils) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:09">[00:39:09]</a>.

*   **Cardiology:**
    *   Heart failure: Beta-blockers for reducing mortality (Metoprolol, Bisoprolol, Carvedilol) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:30">[00:39:30]</a>.
    *   Pulmonary artery hypertension: Treatment (Bosentan, Tadalafil) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:50">[00:39:50]</a>.
    *   Aortic stenosis vs. aortic regurgitation: LVH and increased myocardial oxygen consumption in AS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:51">[00:40:51]</a>.
    *   ECG interpretation: SVT, AFib, atrial flutter, VT (narrow vs. wide QRS, P wave presence) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:50">[00:42:50]</a>.
    *   Chronic constrictive pericarditis: Clinical findings (elevated JVP, rapid Y descent) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:42">[00:43:42]</a>.
    *   Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM): Dynamic auscultation, increase of murmur with Valsalva <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:43">[00:47:43]</a>.
    *   First-degree heart block: PR interval > 0.2 seconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:28">[00:48:28]</a>.
    *   Cardiac tamponade: Diastolic collapse of the atrium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:40">[00:48:40]</a>.

### Session Two Examples

*   **Endocrinology:**
    *   Acromegaly: Screening (IGF-1), confirmatory test (failure of GH suppression after glucose load), poor prognosis with very high GH <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:21">[00:45:21]</a>.
    *   Hypercalcemia: Most common cause (parathyroid related, e.g., parathyroid adenoma) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:51">[00:46:51]</a>.
    *   Hypophosphatemic rickets: Distinguishing from vitamin D-dependent rickets (normal calcium/PTH, low phosphorus, high ALP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:00">[00:48:00]</a>.
    *   Craniopharyngioma: Suprasellar lesion with "black motor oil-like fluid" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:54">[00:48:54]</a>.

*   **Gastroenterology/Hepatology:**
    *   Hepatitis B: Acute vs. resolved infection profiles (presence of anti-HBs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:17">[00:49:17]</a>.
    *   Pseudomembranous enterocolitis: Image identification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:16">[01:09:16]</a>.

*   **Nephrology:**
    *   Renal cell carcinoma: Associated syndromes (e.g., von Hippel-Lindau disease with family history of brain/kidney tumors) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:15">[00:50:15]</a>.
    *   RTA (Renal Tubular Acidosis): Type 1 vs. Type 2 (urine pH > 5.5 for RTA 1) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:42">[01:00:42]</a>.

*   **Hematology:**
    *   Iron deficiency anemia: Clinical features (weakness, lethargy, heavy menstrual bleeding), lab findings (small RBCs, low hemoglobin, low MCV, high TIBC), and iron profile interpretation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:35">[00:50:35]</a>.
    *   Multiple Myeloma: Symptoms (fatigue, weight loss, polyuria, tingling sensation) and diagnostic cells (plasma cell on smear) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:00">[00:51:00]</a>.
    *   Hodgkin's Lymphoma: Reed-Sternberg cell, treatment (ABVD regimen) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:44">[00:53:44]</a>.
    *   Amyloidosis: Diagnosis (amyloid deposit identification) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:30">[00:54:30]</a>.
    *   Serum immunoelectrophoresis: Interpretation for monoclonal gammopathies (e.g., IgA, IgM with kappa/lambda light chains) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:43">[00:54:43]</a>.
    *   PNH (Paroxysmal Nocturnal Hemoglobinuria): Diagnosis (flow cytometry for CD55/CD59), clinical presentations (hemolysis, Budd-Chiari, pancytopenia, cerebral vein thrombosis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:02">[00:56:02]</a>.
    *   ALL (Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia): In children with splenomegaly, first-line treatment regimen (steroid, vincristine, anthracyclines) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:36">[00:56:36]</a>.
    *   Post-gastrectomy complications: Macrocytosis (B12 deficiency) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:24">[00:58:24]</a>.

*   **Neurology:**
    *   Crossed hemiplegia: Brainstem syndromes like Foville syndrome (facial nerve palsy, gaze palsy, contralateral hemiplegia) or Millard-Gubler syndrome (facial nerve palsy, sixth nerve palsy, contralateral hemiplegia) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:16">[00:59:16]</a>.

*   **Respiratory System:**
    *   IPF (Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis): Most likely cause of death (respiratory failure due to pulmonary hypertension) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:03">[01:02:03]</a>.
    *   Lung abscess: Clinical features (foul-smelling sputum, clubbing, fever), radiological findings (air-fluid level) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:27">[01:02:27]</a>.
    *   Tuberculosis: Multidrug resistance, role of Bedaquiline (for MDR-TB resistant to Isoniazid and Rifampicin but sensitive to Fluoroquinolones) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:11">[01:03:11]</a>.
