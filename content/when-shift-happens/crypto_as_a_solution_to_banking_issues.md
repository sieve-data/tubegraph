## Challenges with Traditional Banking and Fiat

Traditional banking and fiat monetary systems are criticized for several inherent issues:

### Lack of Control and High Costs
Calling a bank for permission to move money and paying a $50 fee to send a wire are cited as "crazy" practices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a>. If a person wants to withdraw cash, banks question the reason, despite it being the individual's money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>. This highlights a system where individuals lack full control over their own funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>. Furthermore, banks holding money can use it as they wish and take interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>.

### Outdated Systems and Bureaucracy
The current financial system is described as "garbage" and "outdated," burdened by excessive bureaucracy and policy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>. Problems like wires getting lost, with money sitting in limbo, exemplify this inefficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>. The sheer number of middlemen involved in transactions is deemed ridiculous <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>.

### The "Scam" of Fiat Money and Inflation
Fiat money is presented as a "massive scam" uncovered over the last 50 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>. It is argued that fiat money is created "out of nothing" and exchanged for real assets, with added interest rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:47">[00:18:47]</a>. Inflation is characterized as a "hidden tax" that devalues currency by printing more money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:17">[00:19:17]</a>. When large amounts of fiat currency are injected into the system, it devalues the energy and work of individuals who expended their life force to earn money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:55">[00:19:55]</a>. This leads to "wage deflation," where everything becomes more expensive, but salaries do not keep pace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:13">[00:25:13]</a>.

Money is fundamentally energy that can neither be created nor destroyed, only transformed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:21">[00:19:21]</a>. Fiat money, by contrast, is created through simple "keyboard strokes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:16">[00:21:16]</a>. This contrasts sharply with assets like gold or Bitcoin, which require significant energy input to produce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:22">[00:22:22]</a>.

### Centralized Power and Lack of Accountability
The centralization of finance leads to a centralization of power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>. A small group of people controls the global agenda, deciding on wars and major projects without public choice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:16">[00:26:16]</a>. Governments printing money are accountable to no one, and the money often goes to those they favor, leading to corruption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:15">[00:24:15]</a>. Taxes are described as an illusion, a form of "theft" to prevent revolt, rather than a genuine funding mechanism for governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:13">[00:28:13]</a>. Personal tax money going to fund drone bombings in the Middle East is given as an example of a lack of control over how one's money is used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:13">[00:29:13]</a>.

Traditional financial institutions are likened to the Holy Roman Empire, using "finance as a weapon for mass control" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:02">[00:37:02]</a>. The "middleman" (bank, pope) becomes a point of control, and they will fight to maintain their status <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:00">[00:38:00]</a>.

### The Illusion of Success and Freedom
The current system creates an "infinite Tsukuyomi," an illusion where people feel they are living in a utopia but are actually glorified slaves, working willingly for 70 years in the hope of retirement, but never truly achieving financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:39">[00:33:39]</a>. This system makes people terrified to break out, especially due to concerns like healthcare insurance and loans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:01">[00:34:01]</a>. Society dictates a definition of success that is often about looking successful rather than being successful, trapping individuals in a cycle of consumerism and external validation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:44">[00:40:44]</a>.

## Crypto as the Solution

Crypto is presented as a direct answer to these systemic issues, offering a path to individual freedom and a more equitable financial landscape.

### Permissionless and True Ownership
The core beauty of [[crypto_and_blockchains_role_in_global_finance | being on-chain]] is its permissionless nature; individuals do not need to ask anyone for anything, as "code is the ruler" instead of whimsical laws from central authorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. For the first time, individuals can truly own, create, and send their own money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:18">[00:07:18]</a>. This ideological paradigm shift allows people to directly control their finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>.

### Eliminating Middlemen and Reducing Costs
Crypto's peer-to-peer transaction model is hailed as the "single greatest revolution" for cutting out intermediaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>. This significantly reduces the "theft" that occurs in traditional transactions through taxes and fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>. For example, sending money to a friend in the US can cost 10-20% and take days in the traditional system, while crypto aims to eliminate such inefficiencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.

### Money as Energy and Fixed Supply
Bitcoin is seen as "digital coinage" that resurfaces peer-to-peer transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:12">[00:10:12]</a>. Its value is directly proportional to the energy expended to mine it, creating a sound form of money unlike fiat, which can be printed without energy input <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a>. The fundamental theory that money is energy applies to all forms, but only with sufficient energy infusion does money gain value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:30">[00:21:30]</a>.

### Accountability and Transparency
In decentralized financial systems, there is greater accountability. Projects are accountable to their communities regarding how tokens are mined and used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:37">[00:26:37]</a>. This allows the community to be directly involved in the creation of money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:59">[00:26:59]</a>. Crypto is built on code, which is transparent and open-source, contrasting with thousands of unread paperwork policies in traditional finance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:28">[00:11:28]</a>.

### Building a Parallel Financial Universe
Crypto offers the chance to build a "parallel financial universe" where people can transact, buy cars, houses, and start companies using decentralized technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:53">[01:58:53]</a>. This is seen as the only way to escape the "infinite Tsukuyomi" of the traditional system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:16">[01:59:16]</a>. Achieving this requires a "critical mass" of hundreds of millions of people using decentralized technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:02">[01:59:02]</a>.

### Inclusivity and Opportunity
The crypto space is highlighted as the most inclusive environment for building, where value is based on contribution rather than traditional credentials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:23">[01:09:23]</a>. Individuals can get involved in building projects, even if they can't message a CEO in traditional markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:25">[01:27:25]</a>. Founders are often accessible through Discord, Twitter, and communities, allowing direct involvement and understanding of projects at a deeper level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:43">[01:27:43]</a>. ### Investing in Crypto for Longevity
For investors, the key is to bet on quality teams and narratives, especially during bear markets when prices are depressed and opportunities are more plentiful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:55">[01:19:55]</a>. Avoiding "getting wrecked" means choosing low-risk, high-reward opportunities, often involving low market cap projects with strong teams and narratives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:07">[01:40:07]</a>.
