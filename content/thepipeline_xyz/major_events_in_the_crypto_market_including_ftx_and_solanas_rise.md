## Key Market Narratives and the Future of Crypto

### Layer 1 and Layer 2 Competition

The [[crypto_ecosystem_developments | Layer 1 (L1)]] narrative remains strong, exemplified by [[solana_and_blockchain_development | Solana]] and Avalanche (AVAX) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:38:00">[20:38:00]</a>. These L1s offer advantages over Ethereum's mainnet in terms of lower costs, faster speeds, and better user experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:52:00">[20:52:00]</a>. Layer 2 (L2) solutions are also a significant narrative, continually working to compete with L1s like Solana on metrics like transactions per second (TPS), user experience, and fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:18:00">[21:18:00]</a>. The EIP-4844 upgrade is expected to make Ethereum rollups significantly cheaper <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:23:00">[22:23:00]</a>.

Users, particularly retail participants, are not loyal to specific chains; they prioritize ease of use and efficient applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:45:00">[22:45:00]</a>. This competitive landscape will continue to drive innovation in onboarding users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:41:00">[22:41:00]</a>.

### Early Stages of Mass Adoption

Despite crypto being around since 2009, the infrastructure to support large-scale, consumer-facing applications has only recently become available, notably with [[solana_and_blockchain_development | Solana]] gaining prominence around September 2021 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:51:00">[25:51:00]</a>. Previously, the internet itself lacked the necessary speed and bandwidth for modern applications like social media. Similarly, true mass adoption for crypto applications requires underlying layers that can handle millions of daily active users and high transaction volumes at low costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:29:00">[24:29:00]</a>.

Anom believes that as crypto develops more real-world applications and integrates with larger companies (e.g., DePIN projects), it will become easier to distinguish genuine value from purely narrative-driven projects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:30:00">[19:30:00]</a>. This current phase is comparable to the early 2000s for tech companies, where significant value emerged over time despite initial failures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:07:00">[27:07:00]</a>.

### Promising Application Areas

#### Apps as "Main Character"

Kevin believes that crypto applications (`apps`) will become the "main character" in the next two years, following the L1 focus of previous cycles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:30:00">[29:30:00]</a>. The first successful apps at scale are expected to have an unprecedented impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:39:00">[29:39:00]</a>.

#### Payments and Stablecoins

Stablecoins are identified as a massive market with a uniquely enabled solution by crypto <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:21:00">[32:21:00]</a>. Leveraging the original vision of Bitcoin as peer-to-peer cash, crypto payment rails offer objective advantages over traditional systems like Visa and MasterCard, which charge high fees for transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:37:00">[32:37:00]</a>. Innovations like yield-bearing stablecoins (e.g., Mountain Protocol, Ondo Finance) can make crypto payments superior to traditional options like Venmo by offering T-Bill interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:19:00">[33:19:00]</a>. The existing adoption of Tether in countries in Africa and South America for everyday payments demonstrates this market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:05:00">[34:05:00]</a>.

#### Social and Gaming

[[social_media_and_community_marketing_in_crypto | Social]] applications hold significant interest due to the potential for better incentive alignment compared to traditional social media, where users are often the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:35:00">[31:35:00]</a>.

Gaming is another major area. Drip House on Solana, a free NFT platform allowing artists to release art weekly using compressed NFTs, is a successful example of onboarding new users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:44:00">[30:44:00]</a>. Users often transition from Drip House to other DeFi applications on Solana <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:21:00">[31:21:00]</a>.

The challenge lies in overcoming the "Normie perception" that NFTs and crypto are scams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:25:00">[36:25:00]</a>. Anom believes a good app, even a "Trojan horse" type that doesn't explicitly highlight its crypto backend, can break through <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:36:00">[37:36:00]</a>. Parallel, a gaming project, aims to onboard web2 gamers who can play without needing a crypto address but can opt to own in-game assets as NFTs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:01:00">[38:01:00]</a>. Free-to-use NFT apps like Drip House also mitigate the "scam" perception by removing financial risk for users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:49:00">[38:49:00]</a>.

Anom's thesis for the upcoming cycle is twofold: continued acceleration of gambling (crypto allows easy betting on anything) and the emergence of applications that display the fundamental value of the space without requiring users to bet money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:16:00">[39:16:00]</a>. Sports betting through crypto is highlighted as an area where the product is objectively better than web2 alternatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:01:00">[41:01:00]</a>.

### Preventing Another Luna Event

Anom hopes to avoid another "Luna type event" or similar mechanisms that could lead to easy collapse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:37:00">[48:37:00]</a>. While leverage-driven liquidations and flash crashes are inherent to crypto, the industry is becoming more cautious with designs. The issues with FTX, stemming from a lack of transparency and poor risk mechanisms, are being addressed by newer exchanges like Cube and Backpack, which prioritize transparency and user fund access <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:19:00">[49:19:00]</a>. Many researchers tried to warn about Luna's design flaws, and Anom believes these "smarter voices" will be heeded more this cycle, contributing to [[building_trust_in_the_market_particularly_in_crypto | building trust in the market particularly in crypto]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:10:00">[50:10:00]</a>.

However, the phenomenon of anonymous users raising millions from the public, often by explicitly stating they might "rug it," remains a concern, highlighting the market's current speculative nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:31:00">[50:31:00]</a>.
