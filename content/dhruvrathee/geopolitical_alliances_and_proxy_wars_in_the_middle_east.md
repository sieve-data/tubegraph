## Direct Conflict: Iran and Israel

The direct involvement of Iran in attacks against Israel marks a significant escalation in the ongoing tensions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:00">[01:37:00]</a>.
*   On October 1st, more than 180 ballistic missiles were launched by Iran towards Israel, under the direct order of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>. Iran's IRGC claimed over 90% of missiles hit their targets, while Israel stated most were intercepted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:00">[00:29:00]</a>. Damage included a 30-foot deep crater near Mossad's headquarters and targets near a military base in the Negev desert <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:00">[00:46:00]</a>. Despite Israel reporting minimal damage, Prime Minister Netanyahu vowed retaliation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:00">[00:57:00]</a>.
*   Prior to this, on April 13th, Iran launched over 300 drones and missiles towards Israel, including about 170 drones, 30 cruise missiles, and 110 ballistic missiles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:00">[02:26:00]</a>. This marked the first time in decades another country directly attacked Israel, with the previous instance being Saddam Hussein's Iraq during the 1991 Gulf War <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:49:00">[02:49:00]</a>.

### Reasons for Iranian Attacks

Iran frames its attacks as retaliation for an Israeli airstrike around April 1st on Syria's capital, Damascus, which killed seven Iranian officers, including a top general from the Iranian embassy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:06:00">[03:06:00]</a>. Israel claimed the targeted building was a military facility and the deceased officer had links to [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:32:00">[03:32:00]</a>.

Further escalations led to the October 1st attack:
*   A high-profile assassination of Hamas political leader Ismail Haniyeh in Tehran on July 31st <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:24:00">[15:24:00]</a>. Israel is suspected of responsibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:38:00">[15:38:00]</a>.
*   The "Pager attack" on [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]] operatives on September 17th, which killed many <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:56:00">[15:56:00]</a>.
*   Assassinations of [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]]'s Special Operations Forces Unit commander Ibrahim Akil (September 20th), [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]]'s missile unit leader (September 25th), and IRGC Brigadier General Abbas Nilforoushan (September 27th) in an airstrike on [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]]'s headquarters, which also killed top leader Hassan Nasrallah <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:06:00">[16:06:00]</a>.

These events prompted Iran's October 1st missile launch, which an Iranian news agency cited as retaliation for the assassination of [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]]'s leader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:34:00">[17:34:00]</a>.

### Israel's Defense Systems

Israel utilizes advanced defense systems to counter missile threats:
*   **Iron Dome**: Protects against short-range rockets up to 10 km altitude within a 70 km radius <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30:00">[04:30:00]</a>. It intercepts rockets before they hit the ground by launching Tamir Missiles, each costing $50,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:45:00">[04:45:00]</a>.
*   **David Sling**: Designed for medium to long-range missiles, protecting as high as 15 km in a 300 km radius <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:07:00">[05:07:00]</a>. Each missile costs $1 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28:00">[06:28:00]</a>.
*   **Arrow System (Arrow 3)**: Has the highest detection range (2,400 km) and altitude (100 km), capable of intercepting missiles launched from near space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:25:00">[05:25:00]</a>. Its missiles cost several millions of dollars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:34:00">[06:34:00]</a>.

During the April attack, Israel, aided by the US, UK, and Jordan, spent around $200 million in one day on defense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:40:00">[06:40:00]</a>. The sheer quantity of missiles launched by Iran can overwhelm Israel's defense systems due to their high cost and limited supply <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:51:00">[06:51:00]</a>.

Iran possesses advanced weaponry, including 2023 Fatah hypersonic ballistic missiles, which can travel at 18,000 km/h (15 times the speed of sound) and reach Israel in minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:47:00">[07:47:00]</a>. Their longest-range missiles are the Shahab-3, with a range over 2,000 km <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:39:00">[07:39:00]</a>.

Iran reportedly funds and supports these groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:29:00">[09:29:00]</a>.

*   **Hamas (in Gaza)**: Its attack on Israel on October 7th, 2023, initiated the current war <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:57:00">[11:57:00]</a>.
*   **[[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]] (in South Lebanon)**: Considered one of the most heavily armed non-state groups globally, it gained power after Israel's 1982 invasion of Lebanon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:47:00">[10:47:00]</a>. It became an official entity in 1985 with a manifesto demanding the destruction of the Israeli state <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:57:00">[10:57:00]</a>. Most Western countries consider [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paramilitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]] a terrorist organization, though the Indian government does not <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:08:00">[11:08:00]</a>. [[role_of_hezbollah_and_other_paraimlitary_groups_in_the_war | Hezbollah]] pushed Israel out of Lebanon in 2000 and fought a 34-day war with Israel in 2006 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:21:00">[11:21:00]</a>. It declared war against Israel on October 8th, 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:02:00">[12:02:00]</a>. Its continuous rocket fire has displaced over 80,000 Israelis from border towns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:45:00">[14:45:00]</a>.
*   **Houthis (in Yemen)**: Founded in the 1990s due to Shia marginalization and increasing foreign influence in Yemen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:11:00">[12:11:00]</a>. Many countries consider them a terrorist organization, but the Indian government does not <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:19:00">[12:19:00]</a>. Besides Iran, Russia is also said to fund the Houthis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:30:00">[12:30:00]</a>. ## Broader Geopolitical Alliances

The conflict also highlights broader [[alliances_and_international_relations_before_world_war_i | alliances and international relations]] in the Middle East and globally:
*   **Israel's Allies**: The US, UK, and Jordan provided assistance to Israel during the April missile attack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>.
*   **Iran's Allies/Supporters**: Russia and China are seen as favoring Iran <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:13:00">[13:13:00]</a>.
*   **Regional Rivalry (Saudi Arabia vs. Iran)**: Saudi Arabia and Iran are depicted as two regional superpowers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:43:00">[12:43:00]</a>. They often support opposing sides in neighboring countries:
    *   In Yemen: Saudi Arabia supports the government, while Iran favors the rebels (Houthis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:53:00">[12:53:00]</a>.
    *   In Iraq, Syria, and Lebanon: Saudi Arabia generally favors the government, while Iran supports Shia rebel groups or specific factions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:58:00">[12:58:00]</a>.
    *   This rivalry is partly fueled by their religious divide: Saudi Arabia is Sunni-dominated, and Iran is Shia-dominated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:25:00">[13:25:00]</a>.

Internationally, Western countries, including the US and EU, generally favor Saudi Arabia, while Russia and China tend to favor Iran <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:08:00">[13:08:00]</a>.
