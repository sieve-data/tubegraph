## Life Under the North Korean Regime
Born in Hyesan, a northern part of North Korea, Yeonmi Park's early life was marked by extreme poverty and state control <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:57:00">[04:57:00]</a>. Accessing food was incredibly difficult, forcing her to eat insects like grasshoppers, dragonflies, and butterflies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:12:00">[06:12:00]</a>. Grasshoppers were considered "chunk meat," while dragonflies offered little sustenance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28:00">[06:28:00]</a>. They even consumed rats, with methods of preparation varying based on concerns about tuberculosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:53:00">[09:53:00]</a>. Cockroaches were eaten as snacks, with the "most delicious part" being a grain-like substance found inside their abdomens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:16:00">[12:16:00]</a>. Park states she has "zero allergy" due to consuming "everything" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:05:00">[13:05:00]</a>. Despite the current discussions in America about eating insects to "save the planet," she expresses a strong aversion to returning to such a diet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:49:00">[10:49:00]</a>.

North Korea operates under a strict totalitarian system where the government controls every aspect of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:08:00">[05:08:00]</a>.
*   **Censorship and Propaganda**: Comedy, for instance, must glorify the dictators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:10:00">[05:10:00]</a>. Information is tightly controlled; maps of the world are not shown, and people are told they belong to "Kim's race" rather than "Asian" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:00">[04:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:57:00">[04:57:00]</a>. The North Korean calendar begins with Kim Il Sung's birth, effectively erasing other historical facts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:02:00">[04:02:00]</a>. Propaganda fabricates achievements of leaders like Kim Jong Il, claiming he learned to walk at 8 weeks or invented the "double bread with meat" (hamburger) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:12:00">[42:12:00]</a>. People in North Korea often have no concept of golf or hamburgers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:42:00">[43:42:00]</a>.
*   **Political Control and Punishment**: There are two types of crimes: political and economic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:33:00">[17:33:00]</a>. Political crimes, such as making fun of the president, can lead to the arrest of up to eight generations of a family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:41:00">[17:41:00]</a>. If a high-ranking official defects, tens of thousands of their distant relatives can be sent to prison camps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:51:00">[17:51:00]</a>. Reading the Bible is the "biggest crime" and punishable by execution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:12:00">[21:12:00]</a>. There are three types of prisons:
    *   Re-education camps (less than 2-year sentences) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:27:00">[18:27:00]</a>.
    *   Labor camps (10-year sentences) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:32:00">[18:32:00]</a>.
    *   Political prison camps (lifetime sentences), which are likened to "Nazi Germany's gas chambers" where people are worked to death, often cleaning nuclear weapon debris <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:36:00">[18:36:00]</a>.
*   **Societal Structure**: North Korea divides its population into 51 different classes based on "royalty," i.e., their ancestors' perceived loyalty to the regime. For example, having a landowner grandparent taints one's bloodline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:52">[01:55:52]</a>. This class system determines opportunities, including marriage and jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:52">[01:55:52]</a>.
*   **Daily Life and Amenities**: Even for those with a "relatively fine life" (like Park, whose father was a party member), daily life lacked basic amenities. She had food three times a day but no electricity, cars, toys, proper bathing facilities, or appliances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:59:00">[25:59:00]</a>. Most people were constantly worried about finding food <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:39:00">[07:39:00]</a>. The regime intentionally starves its population to control them and make it easier to govern a smaller number of people, as starving individuals have "no energy to fight back" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:21:00">[25:21:00]</a>.
*   **Sexual Violence**: Sexual harassment and rape are not recognized concepts in North Korea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:57">[01:59:57]</a>. Women soldiers are consistently raped by officers, and if they get pregnant, they are punished, not the perpetrators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:49">[01:59:49]</a>.

## Escape to Freedom
Yeonmi Park escaped North Korea at 13 with her mother, crossing a frozen river into China <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:48:00">[13:48:00]</a>. The border was heavily guarded, but their broker bribed the guards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:04:00">[14:04:00]</a>. Upon reaching China, both Yeonmi and her mother were sold into human trafficking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:44:00">[14:44:00]</a>. Her mother was sold for $65, and Yeonmi, being a virgin child, for over $200 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:20:00">[16:20:00]</a>. This fate was considered a form of survival, as many others would have died in North Korea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:04:00">[17:04:00]</a>. The broker who helped them escape was later executed, and eight generations of her family were arrested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:09:00">[17:09:09]</a>.

From China, Park and her mother embarked on an "unbelievable journey" to Mongolia, crossing the Gobi Desert for 24 hours in -40°F conditions, with only a 1% chance of survival <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:33:00">[19:33:00]</a>. They were rescued by Christian missionaries who required them to learn about the Bible, testing them on psalms and engaging in prayer and fasting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:08:00">[20:08:00]</a>. This was because reading the Bible in North Korea is punishable by execution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:12:00">[21:12:00]</a>. Park later became Christian after the birth of her son <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:46:00">[21:46:00]</a>.

After reaching South Korea, Park eventually moved to the U.S. and studied at Columbia University, where she obtained her citizenship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:01:00">[22:01:00]</a>.

## [[Global political strategies and conflicts | North Korea's Global Standing]]
North Korea maintains a carefully curated image to the outside world. Pyongyang, the capital, functions as a "Disney World" or the "Heart of North Korea," a showpiece city designed to appear normal for foreign visitors and propaganda videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:16:00">[24:16:00]</a>. However, even tourists are not allowed to move freely and are always accompanied by guards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:47:00">[26:47:00]</a>.

The regime's funding primarily comes from selling weapons, including nuclear technology, which they acquired from the Soviet Union in the 1960s-70s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:15:00">[27:15:00]</a>. Their nuclear arsenal is seen as a "trump card," making direct intervention a dangerous proposition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:51:00">[27:51:00]</a>.
