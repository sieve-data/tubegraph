[[missionoriented_innovation | Mission-oriented innovation]] is a concept and approach that seeks to redefine the role of public policy in driving societal change and economic growth. It focuses on tackling complex societal challenges through a coordinated package of policy measures that are ambitious, outcomes-oriented, and involve broad collaboration across sectors and disciplines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:22">[00:22:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:03">[00:57:03]</a>.

## Core Concepts

The approach advocates for a "market-shaping" or "market co-creating" role for the public sector, rather than merely a "market-fixing" one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:16">[00:16:16]</a>. This involves actively creating and directing markets towards specific societal goals, rather than just intervening when markets fail <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:40">[00:19:40]</a>.

A key aspect is understanding growth not just by its rate, but by its direction, aiming for growth that is more inclusive, sustainable, and innovation-driven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:21">[00:17:21]</a>. This links directly to global objectives such as the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:50">[00:17:50]</a>.

## Key Principles and Characteristics

[[definition_of_mission_oriented_innovation | Mission-oriented innovation]] is characterized by:
*   **Outcomes-Oriented and Problem-Picking:** Policies are designed to address grand societal challenges (e.g., climate change, inequality, health crises) rather than starting with predetermined solutions or technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:02">[00:34:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:23">[00:57:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:29">[01:02:29]</a>.
*   **Bold and Ambitious Goals:** Missions should be inspirational, fostering new thinking and clear directions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:26">[00:29:26]</a>.
*   **Cross-Disciplinary and Cross-Sectoral:** They require collaboration across different policy fields, disciplines (including humanities and social sciences), and sectors (public, private, and third sector) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:29">[00:27:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:37">[00:29:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:46">[00:57:46]</a>.
*   **Driving Multiple Bottom-Up Solutions:** While setting clear directions, missions must also enable diverse bottom-up experimentation and solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:20">[00:28:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:01">[00:30:01]</a>.
*   **Risk-Sharing and Reward-Sharing:** Public policy is not just about "de-risking" the private sector, but actively sharing risks and, importantly, sharing the rewards that arise from publicly financed innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:17">[00:21:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:53">[00:35:53]</a>.
*   **Long-Term Horizon and Adaptability:** Missions often involve a long and sequential process, requiring flexibility and continuous engagement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:15">[01:04:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:40">[01:22:40]</a>.

## Challenges in Implementation

Implementing [[missionoriented_innovation | mission-oriented innovation]] in the [[innovation_in_the_public_sector | public sector]] faces several challenges:
*   **Siloed Structures:** Government departments and agencies often operate in silos, making cross-departmental collaboration difficult due to existing incentive structures and HR processes that discourage breaking silos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:17">[00:41:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:17">[00:42:17]</a>.
*   **Traditional Metrics and Evaluation:** Current evaluation methods (e.g., cost-benefit analysis) may not capture the dynamic spillovers and broader [[impact_of_mission_oriented_innovation_on_society | impact of mission-oriented innovation on society]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:14">[00:35:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:40">[01:05:40]</a>.
*   **Risk Aversion:** Public agencies are traditionally seen as stabilizing forces rather than risk-takers, and decision-makers fear negative evaluations for failures, hindering necessary experimentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:04">[00:48:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:12">[00:49:12]</a>.
*   **Narrative and Mindset Shift:** A fundamental change in the storytelling about public policy is required, moving away from the idea that public action is merely about fixing market failures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:50">[00:20:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:27">[00:39:27]</a>.
*   **Defining Missions:** Challenges include ensuring missions are genuinely broad and impactful, avoiding "mission washing," and allowing for an iterative process of definition rather than a one-off event <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:51">[01:03:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:06">[01:04:06]</a>.
*   **Scalability and Diffusion:** Many [[missionoriented_innovation | mission-oriented innovation]] policies remain pilots; scaling up successful local solutions and ensuring their diffusion is a significant hurdle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:46">[01:01:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:06">[01:06:06]</a>.
*   **Bureaucracy and Administrative Instruments:** Existing procurement processes, budget allocations, and administrative rules can break up the mission logic and prevent timely, coordinated action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:32">[01:08:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:36">[01:33:36]</a>.

## Strategies for Implementation

To operationalize [[missionoriented_innovation | mission-oriented innovation]] and overcome challenges:
*   **Placement of Missions:** Missions should be at the center of economic growth strategies, not just siloed within innovation policy, engaging finance ministries alongside innovation ministries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:50">[00:41:50]</a>.
*   **Co-Creation and Participation:** Engage diverse stakeholders, including citizens, from the outset to co-create missions and solutions, ensuring bottom-up input <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:05">[00:28:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:01">[01:22:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:37">[01:42:37]</a>.
*   **Place-Based Approach:** Focus on specific system environments (like a street or a school) to foster systemic change, enabling direct work with people in context and immediately addressing complexity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:50">[01:13:50]</a>.
*   **Platform Strategy:** Align the entire system around prototypes and mission areas, fostering collaboration and coordination across different initiatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:44">[01:22:44]</a>.
*   **Building Capability and Learning Organizations:** Public sector organizations need internal capabilities to take risks, learn from failures, and continuously adapt, moving away from an outsourcing mentality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:15">[00:49:15]</a>.
*   **Dynamic Metrics and Evaluation:** Develop new metrics that capture dynamic spillovers and societal impact, moving beyond traditional cost-benefit analysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:14">[00:35:14]</a>.
*   **Strategic Use of Public Finance and Procurement:** Leverage public banks, procurement agencies, and recovery funds to steer the direction of growth, setting conditionalities that encourage innovation and public value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:26">[00:32:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:04">[00:37:04]</a>.
*   **Focus on Relationships:** Recognize that building effective collaboration and breaking silos is fundamentally about fostering trusted personal relationships and mutual understanding among diverse actors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:44">[02:04:44]</a>.

## Examples and Case Studies

*   **European Commission:** [[Missionoriented innovation | Missions]] have been put at the core of the ambitious 100 billion euro proposal for [[missionoriented_innovation | mission-oriented innovation]] research and innovation in the European Union <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>.
*   **Moon Landing:** The Apollo program serves as a historical inspiration for [[missionoriented_innovation | mission-oriented innovation]], demonstrating the power of a bold, ambitious goal to drive cross-sectoral collaboration and foster innovation in various fields (e.g., materials, electronics, software) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:49">[00:26:49]</a>.
*   **Sweden (Vinnova):**
    *   **Sustainable Streets:** A mission to use existing streets to produce healthy, sustainable, vibrant environments. This involved local engagement, including school children as urban designers, and brought together diverse actors to transform parking spaces into green, social areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:50">[01:14:50]</a>.
    *   **School Food System:** Orienting the public meal system (2 million meals/day) away from just cost and hygiene towards sustainability, health, and regenerative agriculture, involving a wide range of organizations and leading to new recipes and procurement calls for municipalities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:14">[01:20:14]</a>.
*   **Netherlands (Health~Holland):** The central mission of [[missionoriented_innovation in healthcare | health and care]] aims for longer, healthier lives for Dutch citizens and decreasing health inequalities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:49">[01:40:49]</a>. This involves a "quadruple helix" approach, bringing together researchers, entrepreneurs, government, and citizens in co-creation processes, exemplified by initiatives like Tom de Luisa for people with dementia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:40">[01:41:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:10">[01:45:10]</a>.
*   **Spain (ENISA):** ENISA, an agency under the Ministry of Industry, focuses on supporting entrepreneurs and innovative SMEs in four pilot areas: smart industry, gender equality, territorial balance, and impact investment. Missions include redesigning mobility, universal access to housing, secure health, and rural agri-food activation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:31">[01:25:31]</a>.
*   **UK (Royal Academy of Engineering - Engineering X):** Engineering X seeks to use engineering expertise to tackle global challenges like safer end-of-engineered-life (e.g., ship disposal, electronic waste). *   **Italy (Recovery Scheme):** Italy's recovery scheme under the European plan focuses on investments in climate and digitalization, with ongoing work to build public administration capacity for implementing these changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:02">[00:34:02]</a>.
