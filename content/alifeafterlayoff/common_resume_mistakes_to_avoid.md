## Design and Formatting Mistakes

### Bright Graphics or Colors
Avoid using bright graphics, charts, or clip art <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:00">[00:56:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:00">[01:22:00]</a>. A resume should be a professional document, and excessive color can distract the reviewer from your qualifications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:00">[01:26:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> Stick to simple colors like blues, blacks, or possibly magenta for minor accents like a job title <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:00">[01:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:00">[01:05:00]</a>. Keep it clean and concise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:00">[01:13:00]</a>.

> [!NOTE]
> The only exception is if you are in a creative field (e.g., art director, graphic designer) where such elements are expected to showcase your creativity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:00">[01:35:00]</a>.

### Infographics for Skill Proficiency
Do not use infographics (e.g., bar charts) to demonstrate skill proficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:00">[02:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:00">[02:09:00]</a>. Showing less than full proficiency can actually highlight a weakness in a skill that might be crucial to the employer, potentially eliminating you from consideration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:00">[02:17:00]</a>. It is better to let your proficiency be assessed during the interview process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:00">[02:34:00]</a>.

### Columns
Avoid using multiple columns on your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:00">[02:54:00]</a>. Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) typically parse information from left to right, and while most are sophisticated, they can struggle with columns, potentially missing valuable skills listed in other columns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:00">[02:55:00]</a>. This can make you invisible to recruiters searching for keywords <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> Eliminate columns altogether to ensure all your skills are discoverable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a>.

## Personal Information Mistakes

### Photographs
In the U.S. and North America, avoid including a photograph of yourself on your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:00">[03:54:00]</a>. Its presence might lead recruiters to assume you are not based in the U.S., and it can also lead to judgment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01:00">[04:01:00]</a>.

> [!NOTE]
> If applying in a country where photographs are customary, follow local traditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:15:00">[04:15:00]</a>.

### Personal Identifiers
Do not include personal identifiers such as gender, race, creed, color, or marital status (e.g., "married with two kids") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>. This information is irrelevant to your work qualifications and can be used by unscrupulous employers to discriminate against you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:35:00">[04:35:00]</a>.

> [!WARNING]
> Omit anything that pertains to a protected class to prevent discrimination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>. This tip is U.S.-specific, but generally, avoid unnecessary personal details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a>.

### Unprofessional Email Addresses
Ensure your email address is professional <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:14:00">[05:14:00]</a>. Avoid "colorful" or old email addresses from college years (e.g., "Iusuallyparty@yahoo.com") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:24:00">[05:24:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> Create a clean, neutral email address, such as `yourfullname@gmail.com` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:33:00">[05:33:00]</a>.

### Full Addresses
Only include your city and state on your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:53:00">[05:53:00]</a>. Full street addresses are unnecessary and can lead to potential discrimination based on commute distance or geographical location (e.g., taxation issues for remote roles) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:03:00">[06:03:00]</a>.

> [!NOTE]
> For remote positions, still include your city and state. This helps recruiters understand time zones and ensure the company is set up to do business in your region <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:48:00">[06:48:00]</a>.

## Content and Experience Mistakes

### Irrelevant Work History
Limit your work history to the most recent 10 years, as employers generally focus on this period (or even the last five) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:35:00">[07:35:00]</a>. Older skills may no longer be relevant due to market changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:49:00">[07:49:00]</a>.

> [!NOTE]
> Only include more than 10 years if the job specifically requires extensive experience, typically senior executive roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:58:00">[07:58:00]</a>.

### Industry-Specific Jargon or Acronyms
Avoid using industry-specific jargon or acronyms that a recruiter or hiring manager outside your specific company might not understand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:12:00">[08:12:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> Make your resume as clear and understandable as possible to maximize your chances of being considered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:31:00">[08:31:00]</a>.

### Excessive Education Detail
Do not provide excessive detail about your degrees, such as the specific business school within a university (e.g., "Schmiel" at Penn State) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:46:00">[08:46:00]</a>. Minor degrees are good keywords but do not require significant "real estate" on your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:00">[09:00:00]</a>.

> [!NOTE]
> If you are entry-level, including more detailed courses or keywords might be beneficial for your first job out of college <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:11:00">[09:11:00]</a>.

### GPA
If you are more than three years out of college, remove your GPA from your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:19:00">[09:19:00]</a>. Employers generally do not care about it after a certain point in your career <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:25:00">[09:25:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> Keep your GPA if you are entry-level or have less than three years of total work experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:37:00">[09:37:00]</a>.

### Internships (for experienced professionals)
If you have been in the workforce for longer than three years, you can remove internships from your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:45:00">[09:45:00]</a>. They are not typically counted as total years of experience and become "vanity stats" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:51:00">[09:51:00]</a>.

### References / "References Available Upon Request"
Do not list actual references or state "references available upon request" on your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:00">[09:56:00]</a>. Recruiters will ask for references if needed, and reference checks are becoming less common <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:04:00">[10:04:00]</a>.

### Salary History or Expectations
Never include your previous salary history or expected compensation on your resume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:37:00">[10:37:00]</a>. Disclosing this information can lead to a lowball offer if the employer has a higher salary range <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:47:00">[10:47:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> This information will typically be requested in the first round of interviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:00">[11:01:00]</a>.

## Skill-Related Mistakes

### Universal or Generic Skills
Avoid listing common, universal skills like Microsoft Word, Excel, or PowerPoint <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:12:00">[11:12:00]</a>. These are generally assumed for professional-level jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:18:00">[11:18:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> If a recruiter needs to know about these basic skills, they will ask in the interview <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:35:00">[11:35:00]</a>. Similarly, for skills like leadership and organization, it's better to demonstrate them through your bullet points rather than just listing them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.

### Generic or Clich Jargon
Do not use generic or clich jargon like "team player" or "fast learner" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:51:00">[11:51:00]</a>. These phrases do not differentiate you, as no one would admit to not possessing these qualities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:01:00">[12:01:00]</a>.

> [!TIP]
> Save valuable resume space for more impactful and specific information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:11:00">[12:11:00]</a>.
