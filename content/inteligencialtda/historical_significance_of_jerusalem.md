Jerusalem is considered the most impactful city in human history, often referred to as the "city of faith" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>. It boasts over 4,000 years of recorded history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:30">[00:05:30]</a>, serving as the cradle of civilizations and a central reference point for monotheistic traditions, influencing over 4 billion people worldwide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>. Its unique concentration of historical importance makes it arguably the single most important city to understand humanity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

## Early History and Foundations
The city's history can be traced back to approximately 2000-1800 BCE <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>. At that time, it was known as Salem and had a king named Melchizedek, whom Abraham recognized as a priest of the Most High God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>. Archeological findings in the southern part of Jerusalem's City of David may relate to this era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:04">[00:08:04]</a>.

After Abraham's time, Jerusalem was occupied by the Jebusites and became known as the City of Jebus or Jerusalem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:08">[00:09:08]</a>. A papyrus at Hebrew University, dated between the 10th and 8th centuries BCE, is the most ancient Hebrew text to bear the name Jerusalem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:19">[00:09:19]</a>. Older cuneiform writings from the 14th century BCE also mention the city <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>.

### Davidic and Solomonic Eras
When the monarchy of Israel began in the 10th century BCE, Jerusalem, then known as the City of Jebus, had not been conquered by Joshua or the judges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>. It was King David who eventually conquered the Jebusite fortress in the 10th century BCE <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:32">[00:10:32]</a>. David also purchased the land of Araunah, traditionally believed to be the site where Abraham sacrificed Isaac <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:51">[00:10:51]</a>. While David intended to build a temple there, God did not permit him <a class="yt-timestamp" data="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>. Instead, his son Solomon built his palace and the [[history_and_significance_of_solomons_temple | Temple of the Lord]] on Mount Moriah, the area of Araunah <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>.

## Periods of Conquest and Rebuilding
Jerusalem has endured numerous conquests and destructions throughout its [[the_impact_of_israels_historical_conflicts | historical conflicts]].

*   **Babylonian Conquest (587/586 BCE)**: Nebuchadnezzar destroyed Solomon's Temple and the city, leading to the Babylonian captivity of the Israelites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:16">[00:11:16]</a>.
*   **Persian Rule (538 BCE onwards)**: The Persians allowed the Jews to return and reconstruct Jerusalem, as seen in the time of Nehemiah <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:28">[00:11:28]</a>.
*   **Hellenistic Period**: After the Persian rule, Jerusalem was conquered again during the Hellenistic period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:44">[00:11:44]</a>.
*   **Hasmonean Period (~100 BCE)**: The Jews briefly regained provisional independence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:50">[00:11:50]</a>.
*   **Roman Conquest (64 BCE)**: Pompey of Rome dominated Jerusalem, allowing a local king, Herod, to rule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a>.
*   **Destruction by Romans (70 CE)**: In 70 CE, the city was again destroyed by the Romans, and Jews were prohibited from living there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:18">[00:12:18]</a>.
*   **Hadrian's Era (132-135 CE)**: Following another Jewish rebellion, Emperor Hadrian ordered the construction of a pagan temple to Jupiter in Jerusalem and renamed the city Aelia Capitolina. He also provocatively renamed the region "Palestina" (derived from Philistine) to upset the Jews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:39">[00:12:39]</a>.

## Christian and Islamic Influence
Jerusalem's significance grew further with the rise of Christianity and Islam.

*   **Byzantine Period**: Under Constantine, Jerusalem became Christianized, emphasizing its connection to Jesus' death, resurrection, and ascension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:32">[00:14:32]</a>. The focus of Christian devotion shifted from the Temple area to the Holy Sepulchre <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:09">[00:39:09]</a>.
*   **Islamic Conquest (638 CE)**: Muslims took control of Jerusalem in 638 CE <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:12">[00:14:12]</a>. Islamic tradition links Jerusalem to Muhammad's nocturnal journey to heaven from the Dome of the Rock (Al-Aqsa Mosque), making it the third holiest city in Islam after Mecca and Medina <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:03">[00:14:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:27">[00:25:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:11">[00:30:11]</a>.
*   **Crusades (1099 CE)**: The Christian Crusades aimed to reclaim Jerusalem from Muslim rule, leading to nearly 200 years of conflict <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:14">[00:40:14]</a>.

## Ottoman Rule and Modern Era
*   **Ottoman Period (1517-1917)**: The Ottoman Empire dominated Jerusalem for approximately 400 years, making it the longest continuous rule over the city <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:44">[00:14:44]</a>. The last time Arabs controlled Jerusalem was around the time Brazil was discovered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:27">[00:26:27]</a>.
*   **British Mandate (1917-1947)**: After WWI, the British took control of the region, which they called Palestine. They made promises to both Arabs and Jews regarding the land, creating a complex situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:40">[00:44:40]</a>.
*   **Creation of the State of Israel (1948)**: In 1948, the United Nations recognized the creation of the State of Israel, allowing Jews to return to their ancestral land <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>. David Ben-Gurion, the founder of modern Israel, fought for this Zionist dream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:52">[00:16:52]</a>.
*   **Ongoing Disputes**: Jerusalem remains a focal point of contention, with few countries recognizing it as Israel's capital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:25">[00:51:25]</a>. Despite UN recognition and international rights, Israel has been attacked in various conflicts, including the War of Independence (1948), the Six-Day War (1967), and the Yom Kippur War (1973) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:31">[00:52:31]</a>. The argument over "who arrived first" and "who stayed" is a central theme in the [[historical_context_of_israel_palestine_conflict | historical context of Israel Palestine conflict]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:41">[00:32:41]</a>.

Jews desire to be in Jerusalem, holding onto the promise that the Temple will one day be rebuilt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:35">[00:56:35]</a>. The Western Wall (or Wailing Wall) is a place of Jewish prayer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:48">[00:34:48]</a>.
*   **Christianity**: Jerusalem is emblematic as the city where Jesus died, resurrected, and ascended to heaven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:51">[00:38:51]</a>. Christians also share the hope of a future return of the Messiah to Jerusalem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:53">[00:56:53]</a>.
*   **Islam**: It is the third holy city, connected to Muhammad's miraculous journey to heaven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:07">[00:14:07]</a>. Muslims believe Jesus, as a prophet, will return to Jerusalem to execute the Final Judgment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:34">[00:40:34]</a>. ## Geographical and Strategic Importance
Jerusalem holds a strategically privileged geographical position, situated at approximately 800 meters high, similar to São Paulo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12:15">[02:12:15]</a>. It is a safe and elevated place with conditions for cultivation and a wonderful water source called Guihon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:48">[02:10:48]</a>. Control of Jerusalem and Megiddo historically granted strategic logistical control over the region <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12:25">[02:12:25]</a>. The land of Israel itself is a vital crossing point in the Middle East, a land of deserts and arid regions, situated between major civilizations like the Hittites, Egyptians, and Mesopotamians <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:19">[01:54:19]</a>.

## [[archaeological_discoveries_in_Israel | Archaeological Discoveries in Israel]] and Biblical Verification
Archaeological evidence plays a crucial role in validating historical accounts of Jerusalem and the [[role_of_israel_in_biblical_history | role of Israel in Biblical History]].

*   **City of David**: Discoveries in this ancient part of Jerusalem corroborate early narratives, including the era of Melchizedek <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:04">[00:08:04]</a>.
*   **Dead Sea Scrolls**: Found between 1947 and 1956 in caves near the Dead Sea, these ancient Hebrew manuscripts are the most important biblical archaeological discovery of the 20th century <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:16">[03:17:16]</a>. They provide insight into the Jewish world of the first century CE and demonstrate the extraordinary preservation of the biblical text over millennia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:20">[03:22:20]</a>. For example, comparing the Isaiah scroll (dated 270 BCE) with later texts shows minimal differences, proving that Jewish scribes meticulously preserved the Bible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26:00">[03:26:00]</a>. Recent discoveries in 2021, even during the pandemic, included fragments of Zechariah and Nahum, further confirming biblical texts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:17">[03:41:17]</a>.
*   **Temple Remains**: Visitors can see remains of stairs from Jesus' time, providing tangible connection to biblical narratives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:00">[03:34:00]</a>.

## Cultural and Social Dynamics
Jerusalem, and Israel as a whole, represents a complex tapestry of cultures and traditions. Israel is a Jewish country but is remarkably open to everyone, respecting Protestant, Catholic, and Muslim sites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:05">[01:06:05]</a>. It's a place where 62 languages are spoken, reflecting its diverse population including Jews from Ethiopia, India, New York, Eastern Europe, Druze minorities, and even Raíte Jews who speak Aramaic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:08">[01:04:08]</a>.

Despite the [[historical_context_of_religious_conflicts_involving_Israel | historical context of religious conflicts involving Israel]] and ongoing tensions, there's a strong belief in peaceful coexistence among many residents. Locals suggest that the conflict isn't between Jews and Arabs, but rather between those who refuse peace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:05">[01:08:05]</a>. The Jewish people, who returned to the desert after centuries of persecution, have transformed the land, creating innovative agricultural techniques like drip irrigation and developing produce like cherry tomatoes, demonstrating a mindset of building reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:34">[01:05:34]</a>. This progress is attributed to a mentality rooted in the Divine word that fosters technology, science, and education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:50">[01:06:50]</a>.
